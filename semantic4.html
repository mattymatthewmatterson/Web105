The 3rd Wave of Ska

By the late 1980s and early 1990s, Ska was reviving into a faster and louder sound. Having influence from rock, funk and even hardcore punk the 3rd Wave of Ska gained most of its popularity by the 1997.

Mighty Might Bosstones

Official Bosstones website

The Mighty Mighty Bosstones originated in Boston, MA and they were originally called the Bosstones. They combined fast metal with horns and ska sounds at the same time. They were very popular on college campuses as well as on television.

Voodoo Glow Skulls

VGS

Although their popularity was mostly in Mexico, the Voodoo Glow Skulls originated from California. Many of their songs are in Spanish but their fast punk sounds combined with 3rd Wave Ska made them a popular in skateparks across the nation.

The Toasters

Toasters

Although they formed back in 1981, The Toasters became popular during the Third Wave of Ska. They continue to tour and their fanbase continues to see them perform live.

Hepcat

Hepcat's Facebook Page

Hepcat is still popular today. Although they gained their popularity duruing the 3rd Wave of Ska, their sound is 100% tradition Ska sounds, similar to Desmond Dekker and Prince Buster.
